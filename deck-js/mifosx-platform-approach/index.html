Mifos X - Platform for Microfinance

A presentation outlining platform approach behind Mifos 'x', the next evolution of Mifos software for MFIs.

Mifos: The Idea

The idea was to '..create and deploy technology that allows the microfinance industry to scale..' and mifos would be responsible for:

  • Delivering a gold standard back-office information management system suitable for microfinance operations.
  • Would be the basis of a new platform for microfinance, which would lead to a way to achieve lower transaction costs for the MFIs, thus serving the poor with lower interest rates and better products.
  • A community-driven open-source solution that can be used, modified and repackaged in any way; The idea being that this could enable an eco-system of providers, located near to the MFIs, that could leverage Mifos right now. (i.e. vendors could leverage the core of Mifos and build their solution for MFIs.)

Mifos: The Challenges

Some of the major challenges that faced mifos and continue to face mifos today:

  • Producing an information system suitable for many MFIs with widely different requirements. (due to differences in microfinance methodology, geographic location, regulatory requirements, size of MFI, etc)
  • Producing an information system that can easily be deployed and configured by an MFIs IT team or through local vendors.
  • Producing/Supporting a 'Model' that allowed for sustainable development of the project to meet the changing needs of the MFIs and industry.

Mifos: Today

  • Pro: Product used in production today

    Based on whos-using-mifos, mifos has been adopted by over 30 MFIs. Several of these are large (>20,000 clients). Some of these MFIs have stated that mifos was critical in enabling them to scale over the last number of years.

  • Con: Wider adoption expected?

    Given mifos is a free open source software, wider adoption might be expected. Right now its not a good fit for the wider community - tends to suit group lending type MFIs. Migrations onto mifos have proved difficult/costly.

Mifos: The Idea (Review & in hindsight)

  • Whilst mifos has certainly helped some MFIs in their efforts to scale, it hasn't become the gold standard back-office information management system suitable for the microfinance industry.
  • Mifos is not a platform for microfinance it is a product (that some MFIs use). A product that has proved difficult to change with no clear way of extending/reusing its core capabilities (note: There has been recent work on adding RESTful api).
  • Have we enabled an eco-system of providers, located near to the MFIs? Can vendors leverage the core of Mifos and build their solution for MFIs?

Mifos X: Return to the platform idea

We want to go back to the platform idea. We feel this can help address the '..many MFIs with widely different requirements..' challenge.

Products built on top of the platform can be in any technology for any device (though a lightweight scripting language like JavaScript seems sensible for rapid prototyping to clients). This gives control to the people who are trying to provide the right functionality for a particular MFI.

Mifos X: Return to the platform idea

The central technical aspect of mifos X is that all back-end capabilities are exposed and easily consumable through a RESTful HTTP API. It is a platform that allows for client applications to be built on/against it.

The Platform API:

The goal of the API is to empower developers to build apps on top of the Mifos X Platform.

  1. Documentation: https://ec2-46-137-62-163.eu-west-1.compute.amazonaws.com:8443/api-docs/apiLive.htm

The Platform API: Feedback to date

Privately and also publically through the mailing list we have received some feedback. It has ranged from a general agreement with the approach and technologies used to more detailed feedback on certain elements missing from api such as:

  1. Pagination: for resources that will require it
  2. Search: improved flexibility on refining search e.g. find all loan by status etc

The APIGEE document referenced in documentation outlines approaches of dealing with this which we would follow.

Whats next?

Whats currently in the platform is a simple implementation of Individual Lending capabilities suitable to a small MFI that worked with us from Jan through to Mar (Thank you Henrik@CreoCore)

  1. Round out extra capabilities for Individual Lending: Fees, Staff/Loan Officer relationship, etc
  2. Other microfinance models such as Village banking?, Micro savings? Fixed-term deposit savings?
  3. Tech bits: make it truly multi-tenant

Lots of possibilities but whats important is we evolve the platforms microfinance cababilities - through collobaration with community specialists with a willing customer on the end of requirements.
